Civil War Challenge Short Story

People’s Choice Awards

 

The Civil War Challenge Short Stories that were submitted to the Missouri Literary Festival are posted on The Springfield News-Leader’s website at www.news-leader.com/civilwarshortstory for public voting. The two (2) stories with the highest vote totals will each receive a cash prize. Voting is open to U.S. residents (excluding Puerto Rico) who are 18 years or older. Employees of The Springfield News-Leader. the Missouri Literary Festival and the immediate family members of, and any persons domiciled with, any such employees are not eligible to vote.

 

Voting: Voting will begin at 8:00 am (C.T.) Monday, August 22, 2011 and end at 5:00 p.m. (C.T.) Sunday, September 11, 2011 (the “Voting Period”). Vote online by going to www.news-leader.com/civilwarshortstory and completing all required information on the ballot. After selecting your favorite short story, click “submit” to cast your vote. Only one vote per valid email address will be accepted. Your computer must accept cookies, or any successor or similar technology, which may be used for the purpose of vote tracking. Ad blocking software on your computer needs to be disabled so that it doesn’t interfere with processing your vote.

 

Winners & Prizes: The First Place People’s Choice Award Winner will be the story that received the most eligible votes by 5:00 p.m. Sunday, September 11th. The Runner–Up Award Winner will be the story with the next highest number of eligible votes. In the event of a tie, the Board of Directors of the Missouri Literary Festival will determine the First Place and Runner-Up Winners. The First Place People’s Choice Winner will receive $100.00 cash and the Runner-Up Winner will receive $50.00 cash. Odds of winning depend on the number of eligible votes received. Except where prohibited, acceptance of any prize constitutes winner’s consent to the publication of his or her name, biographical information and likeness in any media for any commercial or promotional purpose, without limitation, the Internet, or further compensation. Prizes not won and claimed by eligible winners in accordance with these Rules will not be awarded and will remain the property of Sponsor. To claim prize, each winner must contact:

Announcement of Winners: The names of the two (2) winners will be announced on Friday, September 22, 2011 on the Missouri Literary Festival website and at the Creamery Arts Center, 411 N. Sherman Parkway, Springfield, MO 65802. Winners will also be notified at the telephone, number or email address submitted to the Missouri Literary Festival with their stories.
